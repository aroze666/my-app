Frequency

The fundamentals of music are based on simple physical observations.
Definition: Frequency is the rate at which something occurs or is repeated over a particular period of time or in a given sample.
In other words, frequency is how fast something vibrates. When an object is vibrating, the air surrounding follows and vibrates against our eardrums resulting in sounds. Generally, we use frequency as the unit to measure the pitch of a sound.

Harmony

In short, harmony is when many sounds are well combined and played at the same time.

Here is a list of common ratios for two sounds and given frequency example in parentheses.
  • 1:1 unison. (440, 440)
  • 2:1 octave. (440, 880)
  • 3:2 perfect fifth. (440, 660)
  • 4:3 perfect fourth. (440, 586.67)
  • 5:3 major sixth. (440, 733.33)
  • 5:4 major third. (440, 550)
  • 8:5 minor sixth. (440, 704)
  • 6:5 minor third. (440, 528)
    •

    For example if one of our frequency is 440. then 2:1 will be 440 and 880 playing at the same time. In music theory, chords are constructed using the ratios above, but a chord usually contain three or more sounds.
